Why we built it this way

Not the quote, not the invoice, the working document

The quote is the offer, the invoice is the legal record. The order confirmation is what the client keeps open on the second screen during execution: "what did we agree again?".

  • One page, three blocks. Who + who, what we deliver, when. No four-page procurement document; one scroll-free page.
  • Mint green accent, not orange. Confirmation is "go", not "heads up". Mint marks the confirmed scope block and the "Confirmed" pill in the subject.
  • Three planning steps, no Gantt. Kick-off / mid / end. MKB buyers don't want a project-management-software export.
  • References the quote, doesn't duplicate prices. Amount is named; line items aren't. One source of truth for scope.
  • Person at the bottom, phone in the footer. A human, not a support-portal URL.
