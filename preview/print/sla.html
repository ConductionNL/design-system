Why we built it this way

Three tiers, one table, no fine print

SLAs are famous for slick marketing text followed by 30 pages of exceptions. Our version: one page, three columns, one table. What you pay is what you get; what you don't see isn't there.

  • Three tiers, one recommended. Basic / standard / premium. Standard gets the orange "Recommended" pill, because 80% of MKB buyers sit right there.
  • Response-time table with realistic examples. Not "P1 = critical"; "P1 = site offline or data loss". So the buyer knows what falls into which level.
  • Escalation to one name, not a ticket system. Conduction is a people business, not a vendor helpdesk. One number when things go wrong.
  • Out-of-scope explicit. Custom work, integrations, data migrations are separate engagements. Saves three emails per month about scope creep.
  • Monthly cancellable. No annual contracts with retention bonuses. We want clients to stay because we deliver, not because they're locked in.
