Why the deck looks this way

Twelve layouts, one rhythm

A pitch deck has 20 minutes to land an idea. The structure has to do half the work; the layouts are tuned for that.

  • Title ends with a period. Every cover, every section, every closing. A sentence with a period is a finished thought, not a topic label.
  • Agenda has time estimates. "5 min", "10 min", visible in the agenda. Respects the audience's clock and lets them grade your pacing as you go.
  • One orange accent per slide. Title hex, comparison kicker, stats middle column, divider number, accent hex on hero. Never two; the eye knows where to land.
  • Plex Mono on every number, kicker, page-num. Tabular numerals align by default. Stats slide reads like a balance sheet; section divider reads like a chapter index.
  • Mint vs terracotta on comparisons. Same family pair as our prism palette. Mint = good (data we trust). Terracotta = honest about pain. No green/red, no triumph.
  • Greyscale logo wall, one in colour. The wall doesn't shout, the featured logo does. Cobalt outline marks the one we're spotlighting.
  • Closing is "Wat is volgende". Three actions, one owner per action, one date per action. Audience leaves with a calendar invite, not a "thank you" slide.
  • Hex avatars instead of headshots in the closing. Saves slide space, keeps the brand motif consistent, and works even if a presenter joins last-minute (just a monogram).
  • Page-num always bottom-right, in mono. "07 / 12" tells the audience how much is left. Reduces the "is this almost over" anxiety that kills attention.
