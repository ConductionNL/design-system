Why we built it this way

An invoice can be a love letter

Conduction is moving into the MKB market, where the buyer is a person, not a procurement department. Our invoice is read by both finance and the contact person who owns the project, every time. So it leads with thanks, describes outcomes (not hours), closes with what's next. The invoice arrives, the relationship continues.

  • It goes to the contact person too, not just finance. Standard practice on every send: CC the project owner. Finance routes the payment; the contact person gets a note that says "we built X this sprint, here's what's next." Same PDF doing two jobs, that's why personal copy is non-negotiable.
  • Person-voice opener. "Marleen, dank voor de samenwerking..." A two-sentence cobalt-edge panel that names the person and the work. Pre-empts the "this feels transactional" reflex from the contact person, gives finance the context for what they're paying.
  • Lines show what was delivered. "8 hours" is invisible to the buyer; "Vijf entiteiten vastgelegd, schema in main" tells the contact person their project moved AND tells the CFO what was bought. One sentence, two readers, both serviced.
  • Sprint number in the meta-row. Treats the work as a known unit, not an open-ended task. Pairs the invoice with a body of evidence the contact person already has from the working relationship.
  • "Wat is volgende" footer in mint. A future-looking panel for the contact person: what next sprint delivers, or a small ask that helps. The invoice is also a status update; finance ignores it, the contact person reads it.
  • Pay-strip in cobalt-900. The dark band makes the IBAN unmissable for finance. QR placeholder is wired for an iDEAL link when we adopt one, the file is committed today as a LinkedIn QR.
  • Hex watermark. Same comb as the quotation, brand consistency across the documents the same person reads.
  • Plex Mono on every number. Tabular numerals align by default; the invoice reads like a balance sheet, distinctive and trustworthy. The vibe is competent friend, not enterprise vendor — the right register for an MKB-IT-lead reader.
