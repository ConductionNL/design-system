Why we built it this way

One ask, one offer, no three pages of praise

A thank-you email longer than two scroll-heights doesn't get read. What we did, one concrete ask (review/referral), one concrete offer (next phase), done.

  • Three deliverables with mint bullets, not fifteen. Three lines that say "yes, that's right". Moves the client from "what did you do?" to "yes, that's what you did".
  • One ask, orange accent frame. Review or referral. Two things under one request, MKB buyers pick what fits.
  • One offer, no accent frame. Next phase. Separate from the ask, so the client doesn't feel direct-sales pressure.
  • "Keep going." Not "we hope to speak again soon". Concrete, MKB-direct, no marketing gloss.
  • No unsubscribe footer. This is transactional / engagement mail, not broadcast. Reply goes to the sender.
