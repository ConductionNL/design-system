Why we built it this way
Backdrop · the brand under everything

The pattern is the brand. The content sits on top.

Drop any markup inside a cn-honeycomb-bg and it gains the backdrop without writing a line of CSS. Theme + density + accent are the only knobs; the hex shape never changes.

  • SVG pattern, not background-image. Two staggered hexes form a tessellating tile; the SVG <pattern> tiles them across the whole surface, perfectly aligned at any size.
  • Theme over colour list. Two themes covers every page surface. Cobalt for hero / inverse panels, light for body content sections.
  • Accent is opt-in and singular. One accent attribute drops one KNVB-orange hex in a stable spot. Never two.
  • v1 is static. Parallax depth tiers (smaller hexes deeper, drift slower) come later with Motion One. The brand rules already lock the visual language; animation joins when we have a real consumer.
  • Slotted content sits on top. Z-index 1 above the SVG layer; padding inherits the brand's hero spacing tokens.
