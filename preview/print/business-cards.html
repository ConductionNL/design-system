Why we built it this way

The card has to earn the back

Most cards are 85×55mm of identity on the front and dead space on the back. Ours uses both sides and treats the back as the working surface.

  • QR is a LinkedIn invite, not a phone link. People who have your number may not call you. A LinkedIn DM lands and starts a conversation, that's the modern handshake.
  • One orange hex, one place. The accent is the eye-catcher in the top-right. Every other surface stays cobalt, the hierarchy is loud where it matters.
  • Solutions, not skills. Three NL-direct lines on the back, what we build, not what we know. Fits the MKB-direct voice.
  • Monogram as a hex crest. Initials inside a pointy-top hex doubles as a personal mark and a brand tell, pocketable identity.
  • Plex Mono on contact lines. The CLI heritage of Conduction shows up in the typography. Email and phone read like a terminal record, not a marketing label.
  • One small ASCII flourish. A three-line ASCII Conduction mark in the top-right. A nod to the cookie-CLI; designers skip it, developers smile at it.
  • Cut corner, not full hex. A 6mm bottom-right cut, the rectangle that "indicates" via a single hex tell. Rectangles do, hexagons indicate.
