Why we built it this way

The email is a CTA, the PDF is the record

Most "please find attached" emails leave the customer reading the PDF to find the IBAN. Ours puts the amount, due date, and a "Betaal online" button in the message body. The PDF stays attached for the records.

  • Cobalt header band with the logo. Same lockup as letterhead, immediately on-brand. The "FACTUUR" mono label tells the recipient what kind of email this is in two seconds.
  • Highlight box: id, due date, amount. Three numbers any CFO needs to file the invoice. Plex Mono numerals, tabular, scannable.
  • One CTA button, orange, no second option. "Betaal online" with the orange accent. The IBAN below is the fallback for finance teams that route through a bank portal, not a competing primary path.
  • Mint "wat is volgende" panel. Same panel as the printed invoice. The email arrives, and the next sprint is already on the agenda.
  • Signature, not a footer-only branding. Hex avatar + name + role + email + phone. The email is from a person, not a "noreply" account.
  • Cobalt-900 footer with KvK + BTW. The legal block lives in the dark band at the bottom, dispatchable without crowding the body.
  • Inline styles, table layout. Outlook strips <style> blocks; only inline survives. Every cell carries its own declarations.
