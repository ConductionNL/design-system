OpenCatalogi.

De catalogus. Vind, installeer en update apps uit een gefedereerd register van vertrouwde uitgevers.

Stable Cobalt · canonical v2.4.1

What it does

OpenCatalogi is a federated catalogue for Nextcloud apps and public registers. You publish your own apps, or consume those of others, with a uniform metadata spec.

  • Publish and retract apps via a simple UI or the API.
  • Federated discovery, see what other municipalities run.
  • Audit trail per publication, suitable for WOO.
  • Schema validation against the Conduction app spec.
  • Works with OpenRegister for structured records under the catalogue.

For whom

Municipalities and joint arrangements that want to surface their own apps and registers without vendor lock-in. Also suitable for MKB IT leads who want an internal catalogue for team tools.

Status

Production stable. Deployed at 7 municipalities and 2 joint arrangements. Weekly releases, breaking changes only between majors.

Known limitations
  • Federation between instances is best-effort, not 100% real-time consistent.
  • Schema validation is strict; old apps without the Conduction app spec aren't auto-adopted.
  • No CSV import in the UI, that flow goes via OpenConnector.

Why we built it this way

Each app is a chapter, not a brochure

Marketing one-pagers list features and lose context. Ours are paginated like a manpage entry: one app per A4, with the same structure across the family. Stack five fact-sheets, you have the book.

  • App glyph hex top-left, big. 22mm hex with the app's canonical icon. Recognisable from across a desk; the glyph is the primary identifier, not the wordmark.
  • Tagline ends with a period. "OpenCatalogi." with a period turns the title into a finished thought. The tagline below names the verb and the noun: "Vind, installeer, update."
  • Works-with compat-grid. Five small hexes showing siblings; the current app is cobalt-filled, the rest are tinted. Tells the reader "this is part of a family" without a paragraph.
  • "Known limitations" panel in vermillion. Honest disclosure of what doesn't work, on the front of the page, not buried. Builds trust with engineers who would find it anyway.
  • Install strip in cobalt-900. Dark band at the bottom with a CLI command. Turns the install into a copyable artefact, not a sales hand-off.
  • "$ man opencatalogi(7)" running header. Plex Mono running header treats the fact-sheet as a manpage entry. Section number 7, "miscellaneous", reading developers smile.
  • Three pills, three signals. Status (stable/beta), colour family, version. Visible at a glance, no walls of text needed.
