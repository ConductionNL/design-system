Why we built it this way

The envelope shouldn't whisper

Most envelopes are white with a logo in the corner. Ours flip the contrast: cobalt outside, white inside the address window, brand-loud before the letter is even opened.

  • Cobalt as the surface. A Conduction envelope is recognisable from across the post-room. White-with-logo blends in; cobalt with a hex motif doesn't.
  • ASCII sender mark. A 3-line ASCII Conduction box in the top-left, our cookie-CLI heritage stays present even on paper. Recipients who have seen our cookie prompt smile when they see it again.
  • Address in a glassy panel, not just printed. A semi-transparent panel with a faint border lifts the recipient text off the cobalt without losing it, and signals "this is the important part."
  • Stamp zone is dashed, not assumed. A clear region on the top-right respects the postal worker's clear-zone for franking. Pragmatic, not decorative.
  • Hex motif as a tell. 3 hexes (C5) or 5 hexes (C4) at the bottom-right, one orange. Ties the envelope to the rest of the brand without competing with the address.
  • "Aan", not "Address". Same MKB-direct rule as the letterhead. Dutch first.
