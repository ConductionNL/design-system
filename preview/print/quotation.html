Why we built it this way

The price is not the point

Most quotes lead with line items. Buyers scan for the total and miss the value. Ours leads with the outcome (one sentence, three deliverables) so the value is on the table before the price is.

  • Outcome block, top of page. A cobalt-50 panel with an orange edge, "Wat je krijgt" + one sentence + three deliverables. Skim-readable; the lead-in is decided before the buyer reaches the table.
  • Line items show what was delivered, not just hours. Every row has a small "Concrete uitkomst" sub-line. "8 hours" tells you nothing; "Vijf entiteiten vastgelegd, OpenRegister-schema commit-klaar" tells you everything.
  • Real ink space above the signature lines. Each signoff box reserves ~28mm of empty room above its line for a handwritten autograph. Caption + name sit underneath. The paper path stays a real signing path, not a placeholder.
  • Validity stamp as a hex. A point-up orange hex pinned to the bottom-right with "Geldig tot 14 mei" inside. Brand-fitting and physically points to the deadline.
  • Plex Mono numerals. Tabular numerals in IBM Plex Mono on every euro figure, alignment is automatic, the table reads like a balance sheet.
  • Two signature boxes, equal weight. Opdrachtgever en Conduction side-by-side. Not "please sign here", a mutual commitment.
  • 30/40/30 betalingsschema as default. Surfaced in the meta-row, no buried payment-terms paragraph.
